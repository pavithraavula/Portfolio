Who I Am

About Me

I'm a bioinformatician who cares about making data mean something. My work sits at the intersection of genomics and computation — taking raw sequencing data and extracting patterns that are biologically real and clinically useful.

I'm particularly drawn to genomics research and NGS analysis — the idea that the answers to some of medicine's hardest questions are already encoded in the data, waiting to be found. That's what drives my work in multi-omics integration, reproducible pipeline development, and translational bioinformatics.

I thrive when complexity becomes clarity — and I bring that same rigor whether I'm building a pipeline from scratch, interpreting differential expression results, or collaborating across disciplines to turn findings into impact.
