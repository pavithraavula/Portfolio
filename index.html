About Me

I'm a bioinformatician with a Master's in Bioinformatics from Georgia Tech (GPA 3.75), with deep expertise in NGS data analysis, multi-omics integration, and genomic pipeline development across RNA-seq, scRNA-seq, ChIP-seq, and whole genome sequencing.

At Bio-MIBLab (Georgia Tech), I've worked on large-scale genotype–phenotype datasets, developed reproducible bioinformatics workflows in Python and R, and co-authored peer-reviewed publications at ACM-BCB and IEEE EMBC.

I'm passionate about translating complex genomic data into biologically meaningful insights — from raw sequencing reads all the way to clinical interpretation.

Previously, as a Bioinformatics Analyst at a Biomedical Research Center in Hyderabad, I implemented end-to-end NGS pipelines, performed differential expression and pathway enrichment analysis, and contributed to oncology genomics and personalized medicine research.

I'm comfortable working across the full bioinformatics stack — from HPC cluster management and workflow automation to variant annotation, transcriptomics, and scientific visualization with R Shiny and Tableau.
